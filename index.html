MzTEK is an arts organization that aims to encourage women into technical & digital arts spheres, through maker workshops, installations, and other events.

Co-founded in 2009 with Sophie McDonald, we set out to provide fun and accessible workshops in computing, circuitry, and engineering in a way that would engage women and girls.

Since then, MzTEK has gone on to produce myriad workshops, festivals, and installations for the Victoria & Albert Museum, the Barbican Centre, and more, with support along the way from the Arts Council and SPACE gallery in East London.

mztek

MzTEK produces series of workshops to women-only and mixed classes, teaching basic concepts to more advanced techniques.

Participants at a soft circuit workshop sew the Lilypad Arduino microcontroller to their fabric circuits.

The Chi-TEK Tea Party project commissioned and publically exhibited works by women artists and technologists.

The artists presented their work during a "digital tea party" at London Design Week 2011, in the Victoria & Albert garden.

MzTEK teamed up with Guerilla Science in 2012 to produce Human Hacked Orchestra, a digital music & soft circuit project, delivered via festivals across England.
